### 5
The most underrated AI use case in education:

Not tutoring. Not grading.

**Knowing exactly when a student is about to forget something — and surfacing it right before.**

We use a Weibull distribution to model memory decay instead of the standard power-law. It captures an inflection point that power-law misses:

Early phase → forgetting accelerates
Later phase → what survives, sticks

13.7% lower prediction error vs standard algorithms. From math, not hype.

### 6
Hot take: AI grading is the wrong framing.

Nobody wants an AI to "grade" their students. They want:

- Consistency (same rubric, every time, no mood swings)
- Speed (30 seconds, not 8 minutes per essay)
- A human to make the final call on edge cases

The AI does the grind. The instructor does the judgment.

That's the division of labor. Not AI vs teacher. AI × teacher.

If you've tried AI grading and bounced off — what went wrong? Reply or DM me. Genuinely curious.

"Just use ChatGPT to write your exam questions."

This advice is everywhere. And it's terrible.
